Two editions, one experience

Pick the engine that fits the business.

Same intake, same booking, same lead capture. The difference is how the voice is produced — which lets you trade off naturalness, cost, and where your data lives. Not sure? Test both on a call.

Realtime Edition

Realtime

OpenAI's speech-to-speech engine. The most natural, human-sounding option.

  • ✦ The most lifelike voice available — it pauses, waits for the caller to finish, and sounds genuinely human.
  • ✦ Lowest latency — replies in about a second, like a real conversation.
  • ✦ Choice of natural voices (e.g. a warm female or male tone), tuned for a calm, professional manner.
  • ✦ Best for the client-facing front line, where the caller's first impression matters most.

Best for: businesses that want the most premium caller experience. Runs on cloud usage (per-minute).

Cascade / Local Edition

Cascade

A self-hosted pipeline that runs on your own machine or server.

  • ✦ Runs locally — speech, language, and voice all on hardware you control.
  • ✦ No per-call cloud voice fees — ideal for high call volume on a tight budget.
  • ✦ Data stays on-premise — a strong fit for privacy-sensitive operations.
  • ✦ Voice is a touch less natural and slightly higher latency than Realtime — a fair trade for cost and control.

Best for: high volume, cost control, or keeping customer data in-house.

Both editions include appointment booking, lead capture to Excel, your knowledge base, call transfer & after-hours handling, and full transcripts — configured to your hours, services, and branding.

A real booking, start to finish

This is how a call actually goes.

An unedited example: the caller asks for a closed day, gives the address in pieces, and the agent handles all of it — then confirms the whole appointment back.

Live call · Sarah, AI receptionist
SarahThanks for calling Integrity Air Conditioning, this is Sarah. How can I help you today?
CallerI need to set up an appointment — my AC's broken.
SarahGot it — a broken AC definitely needs attention. Is this an emergency, or can we schedule a visit?
CallerYou can schedule a visit.
SarahPerfect. What's your name?
CallerDale.
SarahThanks, Dale. I have you calling from 925-389-1503 — is that the best number to reach you?
CallerYes.
SarahGreat. What's the full service address — street, city, and ZIP?
Caller2102 Foley Lane… Lafayette… Louisiana, 70508.
SarahI have 2102 Foley Lane, Lafayette, Louisiana — is that the complete address?
CallerYes.
SarahWhat day works best for a visit, and would morning or afternoon suit you?
CallerSunday afternoon.
SarahWe're not open Sundays — the soonest we could come out is Monday. Would morning or afternoon work?
CallerMonday afternoon.
SarahYou're all set: AC repair at 2102 Foley Lane on Monday at 2 PM, callback 925-389-1503. Is that correct?
CallerYes, it is.
SarahYou're all set, Dale — the team will see you Monday at 2 PM. Thanks for calling!
